Hampi: The Forgotten Kingdom

April 2018

Hampi is located in east-central Karnataka, India on the banks of Tungabhadra river. The ruins of Hampi is now a UNESCO world heritage site. Hampi was the centre of Hindu Vijayanagara empire Capital in 14th century. The ruins of Hampi are expanded on 4100 hectares. The best time to visit Hampi is in the winter. The only reason I am not mentioning any specific months is because they can be unpredictable. I read somewhere that you can visit Hampi in March also but it was just too much hot. 37 degrees hot to be precise along with humidity. So, to be on the safe side, visit Hampi when it is proper winter. You will be able to enjoy.

The best place to stay will be Hosapete. It is only 13 kms away from Hampi and a developed city also. If you plan your trip in advance, you will be lucky to get a decent hotel also. Reaching Hosapete is not very difficult, there are buses and trains from Bangalore, Hyderabad and Belgaum. It is just that you need to book before or else you may have to pay more than the normal prices.

A two and a half day trip in Hampi went something like this:

Day 1:

We reached at around 07:30 in the morning and got down at Hosapete bus stop. Our hotel was just about 1.5 km away. So we decided to take a walk. The roads were so clean. Our check-in was not until 12 o’ clock, but the hotel staffs were so good that they let us in at around 10 only. We freshened up and had lunch. I did not want to waste the whole afternoon.

Hampi conveyance is dominated by auto-rickshaw only. We took an auto and asked the auto-driver to show us around. They asked for 1000 rupees but if you are good at bargaining you can always bring the amount down. The first day tour was about Vitthal Temple, Lotus Mahal and Virupaksha Temple. There was one brief stop at underground Shiva Temple also. The Vitthal temple complex is a largest in Hampi and is a must see. You would get battery driven cars for Rs, 40 (up and down) to take you up to the complex and bring you back. You need to pay an entry fee of Rs. 30 for tickets. One of the good things is you can use those same tickets to enter into other monuments also. You would see musical pillars inside temple, the stone chariot. Next we went to the Lotus Mahal. The architecture of Lotus Mahal had both Indian and Muslim touch. It is also called Chithrangini Mahal. Its shape from the front is the shape of a lotus.

The Virupaksha Temple is located in the middle of Hampi and is dedicated to Lord Shiva. If you wish to explore Hampi by yourself by walk, it is better to start from Virupaksha. There are buses from Hosapete every 15 minutes. They will drop you at Virupaksha from where you can start your tour.

Day 2:

The second day also, we took an auto and visited Queen’s Bath, Mahanavami Dibba, Pushkarini, Hazararama Temple, Ugranarasimha and Badavalinga and Tungabhadra Dam. Not necessary, the places only mentioned here are the main ones. Every monuments and every ruins of Hampi has its own history. In the tourists, you would see people selling books on Hampi. i bought one with Rs 100 that had names of places to see and briefs along with them. I randomly selected the places i wanted to see and asked our auto driver to take us to those places. The Ugranarasimha idol is the tallest idol in Hampi measuring 22 feet. Any monument you visit there, you will see the precision in the sculpting the artists have done at that time. The beauty of their work shines through even though they are in ruins.

Day 3:

We were supposed to come back that day. We checked out at 12 o’ clock but our bus was at 8 at night. So we thought to utilize that time also. We went to Krishna Temple and I am so glad that we did. The temple was simply beautiful.

Along with Krishna Temple, we also visited – Sasikevalu Ganesha, Kadlekalu Ganesha and the Hemakoota Hill. The Hemakoota hill is dotted with shrines. Both Sasikevalu and Kadlekalu Ganeshas are monolithic statues measuring 2.4 mts and 4.5 mts respectively.

I would suggest you to plan beforehand only as to what all places you want to see and do it accordingly. Do not forget to buy a map. You will be able to plan properly if you have a map of Hampi with you. And also, carrying water in very important. You do not want to be dehydrated. A little bit of planning is all you need to enjoy Hampi to the fullest. The place is really worth visiting.
