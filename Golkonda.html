The Mighty Golla Konda...

September 2018

Being lazy is not easy. Even when you want to do things, it takes a lot of effort to overcome the laziness and finally do it. My last trip was to Hampi and I have been thinking, since I put up my last post, what to write next.

Before visiting Hampi, I had a very short trip to Hyderabad. The only thing I visited properly there is the Golconda Fort as I had a long pending wish of seeing it. It was worth it. As it is only one place I visited, I felt it that writing about it might be boring. But then I thought, why not make the majestic Golconda fort a hero?

I visited Hyderabad in early March this year and it was searing hot there at that time. The day I was supposed to visit Golconda fort, I was feeling apprehensive of the fact that I won’t be able to enjoy because of the heat. To reach to the top of the fort itself, you need to climb 365 steps in total. Before starting with my journey, let us first look at a brief history behind it.

The Golconda fort is listed as an archaeological treasure on the official “List of Monuments” prepared by Archaeological Survey of India under The Ancient Monuments and Archaeological Sites and Remains Act. The Golconda fort is named after its round like structure and if you observe minutely you can still be able to understand the architectural beauty it possessed at the time of Qutb Shahi dynasty. Originally known as Mankal, the fort is in Southern India, covering an area of approximately 11 kms. It was first built by the Kakatiyas as part of their western defense. Eventually, it became the capital of Qutb Shahi Dynasty for the period of 1518 to 1687. The fort was built on granite hills that is 120 meters/390 feet high. The fort was strengthened and expanded by the rulers of Qutb Shahi dynasty over a period of 62 years. Sultan Quli Qutb-ul-Mulk was sent as a governor of Telangana who eventually established the Qutb Shahi dynasty after the fall of Bahmani rule. Finally, the fort was turned into ruins in the hands of the Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb. The region around Golconda is known to have many diamond mines that have produced world’s most famous gems including the Koh-I-Noor. As Golconda’s mines yielded diamonds, it was the main market for diamond trade.

So, we started at around 12:30 to 1:00 in the afternoon. We didn’t want to go too soon to avoid the sun and, we also wanted to enjoy both the sunset and the light and sound show. We reached the fort at around 2 pm and took our tickets. As we were reaching and entering the fort, the sun started slowly hiding behind the clouds and the weather turned into a pleasant one. It was windy, but we didn’t realize how much until we reached the top. From the exterior, you will have no idea how vast the fort is. Once you go inside, it is really puzzling as to where to start. Still we thought that at first, we will be giving one full round of the ground level and then started climbing upwards. While entering, one is greeted by an archway. You can hear the guides telling that if you clap standing below the archway, the echo can be heard till the highest point of the fort. And it was true indeed. We did not hurry in climbing towards the top. We took breaks to admire the view below as we were going upwards. It took us almost one hour and 15 to 20 minutes more to reach the top. As we started climbing up, the ruins of the fort seemed like a map. After climbing further, we realized how much we came up as the people below seemed so small. We reached the top at around 4:30 to 4:45 and the view from up there is bound to take away all your tiredness. The wind was blowing s crazily that you cannot even hear what someone is saying. If you are on the side of the roof, you better be careful or you can lose your balance. As we reached the topmost point of the fort, we could see the entire Hyderabad city. It seemed like someone has spread a white sheet over the city. It was so beautiful and peaceful that I could have sat there forever. We sat on top of the roof for about an hour before returning as we had plans to watch the light and sound show. We came down and booked our tickets. It is always better to be a bit early so that you can grab the front row seats. The light and sound show gives a pretty good and detailed idea of the history behind the grand Golconda fort. My advice for people visiting the Golconda Fort, you should consider attending the show too.

Each walls of the Golconda have a story to tell. You just need to look closely. The fort may be in ruins but the arches, the domes, the halls, the gateways, the temples are still presenting the glorious beauty they once possessed. The Islamic architecture is pouring through every carved stone in that fort.

Okay, so here are a few tips before taking a leave:

  • Make sure you carry water. There is only one place where you can fill your bottle on your way to the top of the fort. but I am not sure if that water is even supposed to be consumed.
  • Use a hat or an umbrella and wear a lot of sunscreen. It will save you from the sunlight.
  • Wear comfortable dress and better not wear sleeveless or shorts as you are going to get a very bad tan, unless that’s something you’d like to get and show off. Also, if you are planning to see the light and sound show there is a chance you will get mosquito bites. Even if they provide you with plenty of Odomos (mosquito repellent), it is always best to take precautions.
  • Last, but not the least, wear shoes. It may seem like just a fort, climbing the stairs (most of them broken) is not easy. Your feet will be in a lot of pain if you do not have proper shoes.
